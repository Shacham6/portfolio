Story Reactor is how I try to *healthily* use AI to help me get into writing.

It's a writing platform with an embedded AI agent that has full context of your story. It acts as a beta reader and developmental editor. Ask it *any question* — about narrative structure, pacing, prose, or character arcs — and get back actionable suggestions.

The editor is built around infinite scroll. Scroll up and you're back at chapter one. Scroll down and you're at the latest revision. Either way, it loads instantly at your current position.

Features:

- A focused editor for long-form writing.
- A revision system so nothing is ever lost. Revisions can be marked as 'protected' to prevent auto-deletion once the count exceeds 50.
- An integrated AI agent that helps you write and get feedback fast:
  - Ingests the full story context using tiered compacting.
  - Offers high-level feedback on the writing as a whole.
  - Gives targeted feedback on specific paragraphs.
  - Suggests edits — great for rapidly fixing typos.
- Uses OpenRouter, so I can experiment with different models regardless of provider.
- A/B testing for comparing different AI outputs.
